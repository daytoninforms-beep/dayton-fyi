Community Voice Network

The Information District produces data, analysis, and accountability reporting. But democratic life does not run on data alone. The Community Voice Network cultivates, connects, and amplifies independent community contributors across the Dayton region — residents who write about what they know and what they see.

How the network works
1
Discovery & Development
Find voices not already in the conversation — not because they lack perspective, but because they lack platforms. Offer publishing support and training through The Journalism Lab.
2
Curation & Presentation
Select from across the network, prioritizing rigor, lived expertise, and good faith over ideology. The standard is need and proximity to root causes.
3
Connection & Context
When the Info District publishes an investigation or analysis, invite network contributors to respond — community interpretation alongside institutional evidence.
Two ways to contribute
Syndicated contributor

Write on your own platform. We surface your relevant work on Dayton.FYI with a link back to you.

  • Publish on Substack, WordPress, or your own blog
  • Keep your audience, subscribers, and monetization
  • Tag posts for Dayton.FYI — we pull via RSS
  • Your piece appears alongside civic data and context
  • Opt in once; editorial team curates ongoing
Native contributor

Write directly for Dayton.FYI. We handle publishing. You keep your byline and rights.

  • Submit via form or Google Doc — no platform needed
  • Editorial review before publication
  • Published on Dayton.FYI with full attribution
  • Licensed CC BY 4.0 — you can republish anywhere
  • Reach the Dayton.FYI audience immediately

Become a contributor

Whether you have your own Substack or just want to write something that matters — we want to hear from you. The Community Voice Network is looking for residents with lived expertise, not credentials. Tell us what you'd write about.

Submitting this form expresses interest only — it is not a commitment. A Dayton.FYI editor will follow up to discuss the network and answer questions. See our editorial standards and terms.

Editorial standards

The Community Voice Network is not the Info District's opinion. The institution's editorial voice — expressed through policy analyses, accountability reporting, and data journalism — remains nonpartisan, evidence-based, and neutral.

Community voices are free to argue, advocate, and take sides. Contributors are clearly identified as independent community voices, not Dayton.FYI staff or representatives.

The standard for inclusion is not agreement — it is rigor, lived expertise, and good faith. Contributors who engage substantively with evidence, who argue from experience, and who treat their neighbors as worthy of persuasion earn inclusion in the platform.
