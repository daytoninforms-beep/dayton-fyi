Terms of use & open data policy

Effective date: June 2026 · Last updated: June 22, 2026

Dayton.FYI is a civic information platform operated by The Journalism Lab, a Dayton 501(c)(3) nonprofit. These terms govern your use of the platform and any content you submit.

1. Open data & content licensing

Our content

All original content published on Dayton.FYI — including news digests, policy summaries, data analyses, and editorial text — is licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) unless otherwise noted. You may share, adapt, and build upon this content for any purpose, including commercially, provided you give appropriate credit to Dayton.FYI.

Public data

Dayton.FYI aggregates, organizes, and presents data from public sources including the City of Dayton, Montgomery County, the State of Ohio, and federal agencies. This data is public record. Our presentation and organization of it is licensed CC BY 4.0, but the underlying data belongs to the public.

Community-submitted content

When you submit content to Dayton.FYI (milestones, events, tips, issue reports, or community voice contributions), you grant Dayton.FYI a non-exclusive, perpetual license to publish, edit, and distribute that content under CC BY 4.0. You retain ownership of your original content and may publish it elsewhere.

Third-party content

Dayton.FYI links to and summarizes reporting from other news organizations (Dayton Daily News, WYSO, Signal Ohio, and others). Headlines and brief summaries are original text written by Dayton.FYI. We do not reproduce copyrighted article text. Links to third-party content are governed by those organizations’ own terms.

AI-generated content

Some content on Dayton.FYI is generated or assisted by artificial intelligence tools, including meeting agenda summaries, data analyses, and plain-language translations of public documents. All AI-assisted content is reviewed by a human editor before publication. AI-generated content is clearly identified where it appears. For full details on our AI use, see the AI Policy in the proposal.

2. Community submissions

What you can submit

  • Community tips: News, developments, concerns, or observations about your neighborhood, described in your own words.
  • Events: Community gatherings, block parties, food distributions, church events, yard sales, cleanups, school events, and other activities open to neighborhood residents.
  • Milestones: Births, graduations, marriages, military service, retirements, community achievements, business openings, and obituaries.
  • Issue reports: Infrastructure problems, code violations, safety concerns, and other neighborhood issues you want documented publicly.

Submission standards

By submitting content, you affirm that:

  • The information is truthful and accurate to the best of your knowledge.
  • The content is your own original work, or you have permission from the relevant person(s) to submit it.
  • For milestones and obituaries, you have a personal relationship with the individual(s) named and are authorized to share this information.
  • The submission does not contain personal attacks, threats, deliberately false information, hate speech, or content that violates another person’s privacy without their consent.

Editorial discretion

Dayton.FYI reserves the right to:

  • Edit submissions for clarity, accuracy, and length before publication.
  • Decline to publish any submission for any reason.
  • Remove previously published content if it is found to be inaccurate, harmful, or in violation of these terms.
  • Contact you for verification before publishing.

Submission does not guarantee publication. We aim to review submissions within 48 hours.

Verification

All submissions require email verification before publication. We send a confirmation link to the email address you provide. Your email address is used solely for verification and is never published, shared, or sold.

Attribution and anonymity

For community tips, you may choose to be credited by name or to have your submission published anonymously. For milestones, the submitter’s relationship to the person is noted (e.g., “submitted by Gloria Williams”). For issue reports, your identity is not published unless you request it.

Removal requests

If you submitted content and wish to have it removed, contact us and we will remove it promptly. For milestones and obituaries, families may request removal or corrections at any time, and those requests are honored without question.

3. Neighborhood correspondents

Neighborhood correspondents are community members who voluntarily bridge information from social media platforms (Facebook groups, Nextdoor, etc.) to Dayton.FYI. Correspondents:

  • Summarize community information in their own words — they do not copy-paste content from other platforms.
  • Are not employees or contractors of Dayton.FYI or The Journalism Lab.
  • Make no formal time commitment and may stop at any time.
  • Are credited as community contributors unless they prefer anonymity.

4. Privacy

What we collect

  • Email addresses: For digest subscriptions and submission verification only.
  • Neighborhood preference: Stored locally in your browser to personalize your experience. Not transmitted to any server.
  • Feedback responses: “Was this useful?” responses are anonymous and aggregated.

What we do not collect

  • We do not use tracking cookies, advertising pixels, or analytics tools that identify individual users.
  • We do not sell, share, or provide email addresses to any third party.
  • We do not require accounts, passwords, or personal profiles.

Data governance

For our full data governance principles — including how we handle public records, property data, and geographic information — see Section V of the proposal. The core commitment: transparency without privacy is a weapon; privacy without transparency is a shield for the powerful.

5. Public records and data accuracy

Dayton.FYI presents public records data (property transfers, building permits, zoning decisions, election results, etc.) sourced from government agencies. While we strive for accuracy, we are not the original record keeper. If you believe a data point is incorrect:

  • Use the “Correction?” link on any item to flag the error.
  • We will verify against the source and correct our records promptly.
  • For authoritative records, consult the original source (Montgomery County Auditor, City of Dayton, Board of Elections, etc.).

6. Not emergency services

Dayton.FYI is a civic information platform, not an emergency response system. If you are experiencing an emergency, call 911. For non-emergency city service requests, contact Dayton Delivers at (937) 333-4800. Issue reports submitted through Dayton.FYI are published for public awareness and community accountability; they are not routed to city agencies unless a partnership is established.

7. Limitation of liability

Dayton.FYI and The Journalism Lab provide this platform and its content on an “as is” basis. We make no warranties regarding the accuracy, completeness, or timeliness of any information presented. We are not liable for decisions made based on information found on this platform. Public data is sourced from government agencies and may contain errors from the original source.

8. Changes to these terms

We may update these terms as the platform evolves. Material changes will be noted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of Dayton.FYI after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.
